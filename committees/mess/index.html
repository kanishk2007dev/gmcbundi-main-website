Official Constitution & Governance

Disciplinary Committee (DC)

The primary oversight body for ethical, moral, and professional conduct within BMSA.

Purpose & Scope

Defining the mandate and jurisdictional authority of the Disciplinary Committee.

The DC shall be responsible for ensuring the ethical, moral, and professional conduct of all Executive Committee members as well as general members of BMSA.

The DC shall have the authority to investigate complaints, issue warnings, and recommend suspension or removal of any member found violating the BMSA Constitution or involved in misconduct.

The DC shall function independently, ensuring fairness, impartiality, and confidentiality in all disciplinary proceedings.

Composition of the Committee

Chairperson

Primary Lead

Vice-Chairperson

Secondary Lead

Five (5) Advisor Members

Advisory Body

Members shall be selected based on integrity, neutrality, and seniority.

Constitutional Mandate: At least two (2) members of the committee shall be female.

Key Functions

01. Supervision

The DC shall oversee the Executive Committeeâ€™s adherence to BMSA rules, policies, and ethical standards.

02. Disciplinary Action

Issuing written warnings to any member violating the Constitution or Code of Conduct. Repeated violations may lead to suspension.

03. Complaint Handling

Examination of complaints related to misconduct, abuse of power, financial mismanagement, or violation of BMSA policies.

04. Hearing Process

Ensuring accused members are given a fair opportunity to present their defense before any disciplinary action is taken.

05. Appeal System

Members facing action may appeal to the Executive Committee, whose final decision shall be binding.

06. Ensuring Transparency

Maintaining written records of all proceedings and submitting a quarterly report to Advisory and Executive Committees.

Grounds for Disciplinary Action

A

Financial Mismanagement

Misuse of funds, lack of transparency, or unauthorized transactions.

B

Abuse of Power

Misuse of position for personal or unfair advantage.

C

Breach of Conduct

Harassment, discrimination, bullying, or inappropriate behavior.

D

Dereliction of Duty

Continuous negligence or failure to perform assigned responsibilities.

E

Violation of BMSA Constitution

Ignoring constitutional procedures or engaging in unethical practices.

Procedure for Removal

01

First Warning

A written warning shall be issued to the concerned member.

02

Inquiry

If misconduct continues, the DC shall conduct a formal inquiry.

03

Final Decision

Recommendation of removal forwarded to the Executive Committee.

04

Replacement

New member nominated or elected as per BMSA election rules.

Special Powers of the Committee

Emergency Suspension

The DC may impose temporary suspension if a member poses an immediate threat to the integrity or functioning of BMSA.

Oversight of Elections

The DC may supervise election-related processes to prevent malpractice and ensure fairness.

Intervention in Crisis

The DC may intervene in leadership disputes, conflicts of interest, or organizational crises affecting BMSA.
